======================================================
FINDJOL: PRODUCT DESCRIPTION (PRESENT CONTINUOUS TENSE)
======================================================


WHAT IS THE PRODUCT?
FindJol is being developed as an offline-first cybersecurity web platform that is helping victims of illegal online loans (Pinjol Ilegal) clear their names. It is combining a local AI identity scanner with an automated OJK dispute letter generator.


WHO IS USING IT?
Victims of identity theft are using FindJol to legally detach themselves from fraudulent loans they never took. Job seekers are using it to clean their BI-Checking/SLIK records before background checks, and legal advocates and NGOs are using it to mass-generate formal takedown requests for their clients.


WHAT IS ITS PURPOSE?
FindJol is automating the bureaucratic process of filing a formal complaint to the Otoritas Jasa Keuangan (OJK). It is transforming raw user input into print-ready, properly formatted legal documents, and it is protecting sensitive KTP data by running all OCR scanning locally instead of sending it to the cloud.


WHERE IS IT BEING USED?
The platform is being used across Indonesia, mainly by citizens who are dealing with illegal lending platforms. It is running directly in the user's browser and on their local device, so it is being used anywhere a victim is needing to file a dispute — at home, in NGO offices, or in legal aid centers.


WHEN IS IT BEING USED?
Users are opening FindJol whenever they are discovering unauthorized loans under their name, whenever they are being rejected from a job because of a tainted SLIK record, and whenever they are preparing official documentation to submit to OJK.


WHY IS IT IMPORTANT?
FindJol is mattering because it is democratizing access to financial justice — it is turning confused victims into organized plaintiffs in under three minutes. It is also protecting user privacy by keeping identity scanning offline, and it is removing the bureaucratic barrier that is normally preventing ordinary citizens from defending themselves against identity fraud.
