======================================================
FINDJOL vs. SLIK OJK — SWOT COMPARISON
(Written in Present Continuous Tense)
======================================================


BACKGROUND COMPARISON
SLIK OJK (Sistem Layanan Informasi Keuangan) is functioning as the official credit information system that OJK is managing to record every citizen's loan and financing history. Citizens are accessing it through the iDebku portal or offline OJK offices to check whether their name is appearing in negative credit records. FindJol, on the other hand, is operating as an independent civic-tech tool that is helping victims respond to the bad records that SLIK is showing — meaning the two products are serving different stages of the same problem.


------------------------------------------------------
STRENGTHS (KEKUATAN)
------------------------------------------------------
FindJol is offering several advantages that SLIK OJK is not currently providing. It is delivering an automated 3-step wizard that is generating print-ready legal dispute letters in under three minutes, while SLIK is only displaying credit data without helping users dispute it. FindJol is also running a fully offline AI scanner that is protecting KTP data locally, whereas SLIK and iDebku are requiring users to upload identity documents to government servers. In addition, FindJol is providing a user-friendly cyber-themed interface that is reducing user anxiety, while SLIK is still using a traditional bureaucratic portal that is feeling intimidating for ordinary citizens.


------------------------------------------------------
WEAKNESSES (KELEMAHAN)
------------------------------------------------------
FindJol is still carrying some limitations compared to SLIK OJK. It is not having official government authority, so the dispute letters it is producing are still needing to be submitted to OJK manually. It is also depending on the user's device performance because the AI scanning is running locally, while SLIK is leveraging full government infrastructure. Furthermore, FindJol is not storing or tracking the user's actual credit history, meaning users are still needing to visit SLIK/iDebku separately to confirm whether their record is finally being cleared.


------------------------------------------------------
OPPORTUNITIES (PELUANG)
------------------------------------------------------
FindJol is sitting in a strong position to grow because illegal pinjol cases are continuing to rise across Indonesia, and many victims are searching for faster ways to defend themselves. The product is having the opportunity to integrate directly with iDebku API in the future, which is allowing users to check and dispute their records in one flow. It is also gaining opportunities to partner with NGOs, legal aid foundations, and Kominfo, who are actively campaigning against illegal lending. The rising public awareness of data privacy is also pushing more users toward offline-first tools like FindJol.


------------------------------------------------------
THREATS (ANCAMAN)
------------------------------------------------------
FindJol is facing several external threats. OJK is potentially launching its own automated dispute feature inside SLIK or iDebku, which is reducing FindJol's uniqueness. Regulatory changes are also threatening to restrict third-party tools that are processing KTP data, even when the processing is happening locally. In addition, illegal pinjol operators are constantly evolving their tactics, meaning FindJol is needing to keep updating its templates and scanning logic. Public trust is also acting as a threat — users are tending to trust official government platforms more than independent apps, so FindJol is needing to keep proving its credibility.
